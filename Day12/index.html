Sleep Science
Rest Better Tonight

Evidence-backed habits that make a real difference to your sleep quality.

🌙

Keep a Consistent Schedule

Going to bed and waking up at the same time every day — even weekends — anchors your circadian rhythm and dramatically improves sleep depth.

🌿

Cool Down Your Bedroom

Your core body temperature needs to drop 1–2°C to fall asleep. Aim for 18–20°C in your bedroom. A cool room accelerates this process naturally.

☀️

Morning Light Exposure

Bright light within 30 minutes of waking boosts cortisol at the right time, setting up melatonin to rise correctly 12–14 hours later each night.

📵

Ditch Screens Before Bed

Blue light from phones suppresses melatonin for up to 3 hours. A 30-minute screen-free wind-down with a book or gentle stretching pays dividends.

☕

Watch the Caffeine Window

Caffeine has a half-life of 5–6 hours. A 3pm coffee means half its stimulating effect is still active at 9pm. Cut off at 2pm for best results.

🧘

Wind Down Intentionally

Create a 20-minute pre-sleep ritual: dim lights, light stretching, journalling, or slow breathing. This signals your nervous system to shift into rest mode.
